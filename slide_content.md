## Slide 1: Taxonomy of Legal AI Agents

Title: A Structured Taxonomy of AI Applications in the Legal Domain

1. Legal Search & Research — sub-branches: Legal Reasoning, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), Knowledge Graphs & Ontologies
2. Litigation & Dispute Resolution — sub-branches: Judgment Prediction, Courtroom Simulation, Argument Mining, Online Dispute Resolution
3. Compliance & Regulation — sub-branches: Regulatory Monitoring, GRC Automation, Smart Contract Compliance
4. Advisory & Transactions — sub-branches: Contract Analysis & Drafting, Negotiation Simulation, Legal Consultation
5. Workflow Automation — sub-branches: E-Discovery, Document Review, Named Entity Recognition, Case Management
6. Agentic Infrastructure — sub-branches: Skills & Plugins, MCP Servers, Multi-Agent Frameworks, Protocols (MCP, A2A, ACP)

## Slide 2: Law Agent Workflow Architecture

Title: End-to-End Architecture of a Legal AI Agent System

This is a horizontal flow diagram showing how a legal AI agent processes a request from input to output. The flow has 5 stages arranged left to right:

Stage 1 — Input Layer: User Query (natural language), Document Upload, API Call
Stage 2 — Agent Core: LLM Backbone (e.g., GPT-4, SaulLM), Prompt Engineering, Session & Memory Management
Stage 3 — Tool Integration: MCP Servers (CourtListener, US Legal, Regulatory), Skills (Contract Review, GDPR Compliance, Legal Research), Knowledge Bases (Case Law DB, Statute DB, Knowledge Graph)
Stage 4 — Processing Pipeline: Legal Reasoning, Multi-Agent Collaboration (via A2A protocol), Retrieval-Augmented Generation, Verification & Fact-Checking
Stage 5 — Output Layer: Legal Memo / Brief, Risk Assessment Report, Contract Redline, Judgment Prediction

Layout: Clean horizontal pipeline with rectangular boxes. Thin arrows connecting stages. White background. Navy blue headers, gray body text. No shadows or 3D effects.

## Slide 3: Legal Datasets & Benchmarks Overview

Title: Landscape of Datasets and Benchmarks for Legal AI Evaluation

This is a structured overview diagram organized as a 2-section layout:

Section A — Pretraining Corpora (top half):
A table-style layout showing 5 major corpora:
- MultiLegalPile: 689 GB, 24 languages, 17 jurisdictions
- Pile of Law: 256 GB, English, US legal & administrative
- LeXFiles: 19B tokens, English, 6 legal systems
- CourtListener: 9M+ opinions, English, US courts
- Caselaw Access Project: 6.9M cases, English, US 1600s–2020

Section B — Task-Specific Benchmarks (bottom half):
A matrix showing benchmarks organized by task type:
- Reasoning: LegalBench (162 tasks), LegalAgentBench
- Classification: LexGLUE (7 tasks), CUAD (510 contracts)
- Judgment Prediction: CAIL2018 (2.6M cases), ECHR (11K cases)
- Question Answering: JEC-QA (26K questions), CaseHOLD (53K QA)
- Safety & Alignment: SafeLawBench, PrivaCI-Bench
- Embedding: MLEB (Massive Legal Embedding Benchmark)

Layout: Clean two-panel layout on white background. Use a light gray divider between sections. Muted teal and navy color scheme. No decorative elements.

## Slide 4: Evolution Timeline of Legal AI (2017–2026)

Title: Key Milestones in the Evolution of Legal AI Technology

This is a horizontal timeline spanning from 2017 to 2026, showing the progression through 4 eras:

Era 1 — Early Foundations (2017):
- AI and Legal Analytics (Ashley, 2017)
- Rule-based Legal Expert Systems

Era 2 — Pre-trained Models (2020–2021):
- LEGAL-BERT (2020): Domain-specific pretraining
- LexGLUE (2021): First standardized legal NLP benchmark

Era 3 — LLM Revolution (2023–2024):
- GPT-4 Passes the Bar Exam (2023)
- LegalBench: 162 tasks (2023)
- ChatLaw, LawGPT (2023)
- SaulLM-7B/54B/141B (2024)
- MultiLegalPile: 689 GB (2024)
- LawBench, LegalAgentBench (2024)

Era 4 — Agentic AI for Law (2025–2026):
- Multi-Agent Legal Systems: L-MARS, GLARE, LRAS (2025)
- OpenClaw + Legal Skills + MCP Servers (2025)
- LegalΔ: RL-based Reasoning (2025)
- Trustworthy Legal Reasoning Survey (2026)
- SafeLawBench (2026)
- EU AI Act Full Applicability (2026)

Layout: Horizontal timeline with a thin line running left to right. Era labels above the line, milestones below as short text blocks. White background. Navy blue era labels, dark gray milestone text. Small circular markers on the timeline for each year. Clean, minimal, academic style.
